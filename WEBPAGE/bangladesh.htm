Do's in Bangladeshi culture

1. Show respect to elders

A common greeting is “As-salamu alaikum” (Peace be upon you). Handshakes are common between men.

2. Dress modestly

Both men and women generally dress conservatively. Women often wear Sari or Salwar Kameez, while men may wear Panjabi.

3. Use the right hand

Use your right hand as the left hand is considered less clean in traditional etiquette.

4. Remove shoes before entering homes or mosques

This is a sign of cleanliness and respect, especially when visiting religious places like a Mosque.

5. Accept hospitality politely

Guests are highly respected. If someone offers tea or food, it’s polite to accept, even if just a small amount.

6. Greet people warmly

A common greeting among Muslims is As-salamu alaykum, often followed by friendly conversation.

Don'ts in Bangladeshi culture

1. Don’t publicly criticize religion or traditions

Showing the soles of your feet toward someone is considered disrespectful.

2. Dont Publicly Show Affection

Holding hands romantically, hugging, or kissing in public is generally discouraged.

3. Don’t point feet at people or sacred objects

Feet are considered the lowest/dirtiest part of the body, so pointing them toward people is impolite.

4. Don’t refuse food too strongly

Rejecting food abruptly may offend the host. If you’re full, decline gently.

5. Don’t interrupt elders

Talking over elders or teachers is considered rude.

6. Don’t take photos of people without permission

In rural areas especially, people may feel uncomfortable if photographed without consent.
