Do's in Sri Lankans culture

1. Dress modestly at religious places

When visiting temples like Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic in Kandy, wear clothing that covers shoulders and knees. Remove hats and shoes before entering.

2. Use your right hand

Use the right hand for eating, giving, and receiving items. The left hand is traditionally considered unclean in many South Asian cultures.

3. Show respect to elders

Sri Lankan culture values respect for elders. Standing up when elders enter, greeting politely, or letting them go first is appreciated.

4. Remove shoes before entering homes

When entering someone’s home or a temple, take off your shoes at the entrance.

5. Ask before taking photos of people

Sri Lankans are generally friendly, but always ask permission before photographing people, especially monks.

6. Try local customs and food

Enjoy local dishes like Rice and Curry, Hoppers (Appa), and Kottu Roti—often eaten with the right hand.

Don'ts in Sri Lankans culture

1. Don’t touch someone’s head

The head is considered sacred, so touching it—even playfully—is disrespectful.

2. Don’t point your feet at religious statues

Pointing feet toward statues of Gautama Buddha or religious figures is considered offensive.

3. Don’t turn your back to Buddha statues for photos

When visiting places like Gangaramaya Temple in Colombo, avoid posing with your back facing Buddha statues.

4. Don’t show public displays of affection

Holding hands is usually acceptable, but kissing or hugging in public can be seen as inappropriate.

5. Don’t use your left hand for food or greetings

This is considered poor etiquette in traditional settings.

6. Don’t criticize religion or traditions openly

Religion—especially Buddhism—is deeply respected, so avoid negative remarks about religious beliefs.
