Do's in South Korean culture

1. Turn your head away when drinking with elders

When drinking alcohol, turn your head away from elders as a sign of respect.

2. Accept things with both hands

Use both hands or support your arm when receiving items from someone older.

3. Follow age hierarchy immediately

Age determines how you speak and act—respect is shown through language and behavior.

4. Celebrate special milestones like 100 days

Celebrating a baby’s 100th day (Baek-il) is an important Korean tradition.

5. Share food with everyone

Meals are communal, and dishes are shared among everyone at the table.

Don'ts in South Korean culture

1. Don’t stick chopsticks upright in rice

This resembles funeral rituals and is considered bad luck.

2. Don’t write names in red ink

Writing names in red symbolizes death and should be avoided.

3. Don’t refuse food or drink too directly

It is polite to be indirect when declining offers.

4. Don’t start eating before elders

Always wait for the eldest person to begin eating first.

5. Don’t call elders by their first name

Use titles like “oppa,” “noona,” “hyung,” or “unnie” instead.
