Do's in Chinese culture

1. Use both hands when giving or receiving

Whether it’s a gift, money, or a business card, using both hands shows respect.

2. Show respect to elders

Age is highly valued. Let older people speak first, sit first, or be served first.

3. Bring small gifts when visiting

If invited to someone’s home, bring fruit, tea, or snacks—it’s a sign of appreciation.

4. Learn basic polite phrases

Simple words like “xièxiè” (thank you) go a long way in showing respect.

5. Practice modesty

Downplaying achievements is seen as polite rather than bragging.

Don'ts in Chinese culture

1. Don’t stick chopsticks upright in rice

It resembles incense used at funerals—considered bad luck.

2. Don’t give certain gifts

Don't give someone a clock, knife, or something with black or white wrapping.

3. Don’t openly criticize or embarrass someone

“Saving face” (maintaining dignity) is very important in Chinese culture.

4. Don’t point with your finger

It can come off as rude—use an open hand instead.

5. Don’t write names in red ink

Historically associated with death or bad luck.
