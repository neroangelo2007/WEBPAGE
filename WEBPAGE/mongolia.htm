Do's in Mongolian culture

1. Enter a ger (yurt) with respect

Always step in with your right foot and greet the host when entering a traditional home.

2. Accept food with your right hand

Use your right hand or both hands when receiving food or drink as a sign of respect.

3. Move clockwise inside the ger

Walking in a clockwise direction follows tradition and shows respect.

4. Accept offerings politely

Even if you don’t want something, take a small amount to be respectful.

5. Respect nature and animals

Mongolian culture values harmony with nature, especially with livestock.

Don'ts in Mongolian culture

1. Don’t step on the threshold of a ger

The doorway is considered sacred—always step over it.

2. Don’t touch someone’s hat

Hats are considered personal and important items.

3. Don’t point your feet toward the hearth

The fire represents the heart of the home and must be respected.

4. Don’t whistle indoors

Whistling inside is believed to bring bad luck.

5. Don’t refuse hospitality directly

Refusing food or drink outright is considered impolite.
