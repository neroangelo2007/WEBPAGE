Do's in UAE culture

1. Dress modestly in public

Wear respectful clothing, especially in traditional or religious areas.

2. Use your right hand

Always use your right hand when eating or giving items.

3. Respect local customs and religion

Islamic traditions influence daily life, so be mindful of cultural practices.

4. Greet politely

Use respectful greetings like “As-salamu alaykum.”

5. Accept hospitality

Hospitality is important—accepting food or drinks is polite.

Don'ts in UAE culture

1. Don’t show public displays of affection

Public affection is considered inappropriate.

2. Don’t use offensive gestures

Rude hand signs or gestures can be taken seriously.

3. Don’t criticize religion or government

These topics are sensitive and should be avoided.

4. Don’t dress too revealingly

Clothing that is too short or tight may be seen as disrespectful.

5. Don’t eat or drink in public during Ramadan (daytime)

It is disrespectful to eat or drink in public while others are fasting.
