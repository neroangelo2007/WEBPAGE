Do's in Taiwanese culture

1. Offer and receive items with both hands

Giving and receiving gifts, money, or business cards with both hands shows respect.

2. Show respect for elders

Older people are given priority in seating, speaking, and serving.

3. Remove shoes before entering homes

This is considered polite and keeps the home clean.

4. Be polite in public spaces

Speaking softly, queuing properly, and showing courtesy are valued.

5. Participate in traditional festivals

Engaging in events like Lunar New Year or the Lantern Festival shows cultural respect.

Don'ts in Taiwanese culture

1. Don’t stick chopsticks upright in rice

It is associated with funeral rituals and considered bad luck.

2. Don’t point with your finger

Use an open hand instead—pointing directly is seen as rude.

3. Don’t write names in red ink

Red ink is linked to death and is avoided for names.

4. Don’t refuse hospitality directly

Politely accept food or drink, even a small amount, to show respect.

5. Don’t openly criticize or embarrass others

Maintaining harmony and “saving face” is very important.
