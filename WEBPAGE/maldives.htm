Do's in Maldivian culture

1. Dress Modestly on local islands

Wear clothes that covers shoulders and knees. Swimwear is allowed only in resort areas or designated "bikini beaches."

2. Respect Islamic practices

The Maldives follows Islam as the official religion. Be respectful during prayer times and religious holidays.

3. Remove shoes before entering homes

It is polite to remove shoes when entering someone's home.

4. Accept hospitality politely

Maldivians are know for hospitality. Accepting tea or food when offered is appreciated.

5. Use your right hand

When giving or receiving items, use your right hand, which is considered more polite.

6. Follow enviromental rules

The Maldives protects its coral reefs and marine life, so follow conservation rules.

Don'ts in Maldivian culture

1. Don't bring religious materials for public distribution

Importing religious items intended to promote religions other than Islam is prohibited.

2. Don't drink alcohol on local islands

Alcohol is only available in tourist resorts, not on inhabited and local islands.

3. Don't wear revealing clothing in villages

Bikinis, very short shorts, and topless sunbathing are inappropiate outside resort areas.

4. Don't show public displays of affection

Kissing or hugging in public is considered disrespectful in local communities.

5. Don't damage coral or marine life

Taking coral or shells from reefs in illegal and harmful to the ecosystem.

6. Don't eat or drink publicly during fasting hours in Ramadan

During Ramadan, eating, drinking or smoking in public during the day should be avoided in local areas.
