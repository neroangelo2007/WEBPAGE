Do's in Bhutanese culture

1. Dress Modestly

Wear clothing that covers shoulders and knees. Locals often wear traditional dress: Gho for men, Kira for women.

2. Show respect in religious places

Walk clockwise around stupas, temples and prayer wheels. Remove hats and shoes when entering temples. Keep voices quiet and behave respectfully.

3. Use both hands when giving or receiving

When offering money, gifts, or documents, it's poilet to use both hands or support your right arm with your left hand.

4. Ask permission before taking photos

Always ask people before photographing them. Photography is often not allowed inside temples.

5. Respect elders and monks

Elders and monks hold high status in society.

6. Follow enviromental values

Bhutan places great importance on enviromental protection and cleanliness

Don'ts in Bhutanese culture

1. Don't point at religious things

Pointing with a finger at statues, temples, or sacred objects is considered disrepectful.

2. Don't touch people's heads

The head is considered sacred in many Asian cultures.

3. Don't step over religious items

Avoid stepping over prayer books, prayer flags, or offerings.

4. Don't wear revealing clothes

Shorts, tank tops, or very revealing are inappropiate, especially in rural areas and monasteries.

5. Don't smoke in public places

Smoking is heavily restricted and culturally discouraged.

6. Don't disrespect national symbols

The king and national institutions are highly respected. Critizing them publicly is frowned upon.
