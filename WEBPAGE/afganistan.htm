Do's in Afgani culture

1. Greet People Politely

A common greeting is “As-salamu alaikum” (Peace be upon you). Handshakes are common between men.

2. Show Respect to Elders

Elders are highly respected.

Stand up when elders enter a room and greet them first.

3. Accept Hospitality

Afghan culture is very hospitable.

If someone offers tea or food, it is polite to accept, even if only a little.

4. Dress Modestly

Wear conservative clothing.

Women usually wear loose clothing and cover their hair in many areas.

5. Eat With the Right Hand

Food is traditionally eaten with the right hand.

6. Remove Shoes When Entering Homes

Guests usually remove shoes before entering a house.

Don'ts in Afgani culture

1. Dont Show the Bottom of Your Feet

Showing the soles of your feet toward someone is considered disrespectful.

2. Dont Publicly Show Affection

Public displays of affection (like hugging or kissing) are not acceptable.

3. Dont Use Your Left Hand for Food

The left hand is traditionally considered unclean.

4. Dont Criticize Religion

Afghanistan is strongly influenced by Islam, so criticizing religion is considered offensive.

5. Dont Photograph People Without Permission

Many people, especially women, prefer not to be photographed.

6. Dont Interrupt Elders

Allow elders to speak first and listen respectfully.
