Do's in Nepali culture

1. Greet people with “Namaste”

The common greeting in Nepal is “Namaste”, often said with palms pressed together and a slight bow.

2. Walk clockwise around temples or stupas

When visiting Buddhist sites such as Boudhanath Stupa, people traditionally walk clockwise around the structure as a sign of respect.

3. Use the right hand

Use the right hand when eating, giving, or receiving items. The left hand is traditionally considered impolite for these actions.

4. Dress modestly at religious sites

Wear clothing that covers shoulders and knees when visiting temples or sacred areas.

5. Remove shoes when entering homes or mosques

Always take off your shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s house.

6. Show respect to elders

Respect for elders is important. Speak politely and allow elders to eat first or take priority.

Don'ts in Nepali culture

1. Don’t touch someone’s head

In Nepalese culture, the head is considered sacred, so touching it—even to children—can be disrespectful.

2. Don’t point your feet at religious statues

Pointing feet toward statues of Gautama Buddha or religious figures is considered offensive.

3. Don’t step over people or food

Stepping over someone sitting on the floor or over food is seen as disrespectful.

4. Don’t show public displays of affection

Holding hands is usually acceptable, but kissing or hugging in public can be seen as inappropriate.

5. Don’t enter restricted temple areas

Some temples may restrict entry to Hindus only, like certain areas of Pashupatinath Temple.

6. Don’t take photos without permission

Always ask before photographing people, monks, or religious ceremonies.
