Do's in Iranian culture

1. Practice “Taarof” (polite courtesy)

People may offer things out of politeness—respond respectfully and modestly.

2. Dress modestly

Clothing should be respectful, especially in public and religious places.

3. Greet politely

Use respectful greetings and show kindness when meeting others.

4. Remove shoes indoors

It is common to take off shoes when entering someone’s home.

5. Show hospitality

Guests are highly respected and warmly welcomed.

Don'ts in Iranian culture

1. Don’t refuse offers too quickly

Due to “Taarof,” refusing immediately can seem rude.

2. Don’t dress in revealing clothing

Modesty is important in public spaces.

3. Don’t show public affection

Public displays of affection are not socially accepted.

4. Don’t criticize religion or politics openly

These topics are sensitive and should be avoided.

5. Don’t use your left hand for giving

It is more respectful to use your right hand.
