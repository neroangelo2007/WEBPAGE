Do's in Japanese culture

1. Bow when greeting

Bowing is a sign of respect and is commonly used instead of handshakes.

2. Remove shoes indoors

Always take off your shoes before entering homes, temples, or some restaurants.

3. Be punctual

Arriving on time (or early) is very important and shows respect.

4. Use polite language

Using respectful words like “arigatou” (thank you) and formal speech is appreciated.

5. Follow queueing rules

Always line up properly in public places like trains and stores.

Don'ts in Japanese culture

1. Don’t talk loudly in public

Public spaces like trains are expected to be quiet and respectful.

2. Don’t point with chopsticks

Pointing or passing food directly with chopsticks is considered rude.

3. Don’t eat while walking

Eating on the go is generally discouraged—eat in proper places.

4. Don’t ignore personal space

Avoid unnecessary physical contact like hugging or touching strangers.

5. Don’t tip in restaurants

Tipping is not common and may even be seen as disrespectful.
