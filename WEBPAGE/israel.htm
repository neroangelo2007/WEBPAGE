Do's in Israeli culture

1. Be direct in communication

People value honesty and clear communication.

2. Respect diverse traditions

Israel is multicultural, so be open and respectful of differences.

3. Dress casually but appropriately

Dress is generally relaxed, but modest in religious areas.

4. Participate in greetings warmly

Handshakes and friendly greetings are common.

5. Respect religious customs

Be mindful of Shabbat and religious practices.

Don'ts in Israeli culture

1. Don’t ignore religious holidays

Shabbat and holy days affect daily life and should be respected.

2. Don’t make assumptions about identity

Israel is diverse—avoid stereotypes.

3. Don’t be overly formal

Too much formality can feel distant in social settings.

4. Don’t interrupt conversations

Even though communication is direct, respect speaking turns.

5. Don’t disrespect religious sites

Modest behavior is required in sacred places.
