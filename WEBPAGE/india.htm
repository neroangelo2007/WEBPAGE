Do's in Indian culture

1. Greet people with “Namaste”

The common greeting in Nepal is “Namaste”, often said with palms pressed together and a slight bow.

2. Remove shoes before entering homes and temples

When entering a house or temple, take off your shoes at the door. This keeps the space clean and respectful.

3. Use the right hand

Use the right hand when eating, giving, or receiving items. The left hand is traditionally considered impolite for these actions.

4. Accept food or drinks when offered

Hospitality is very important. If someone offers tea or snacks, it’s polite to accept at least a little.

6. Show respect to elders

Respect for elders is important. Speak politely and allow elders to eat first or take priority.

Don'ts in Nepali culture

1. Don’t use the left hand for giving or eating

The left hand is traditionally considered unclean, so avoid using it to pass food, money, or gifts.

2. Don’t point your feet at religious statues

Pointing feet toward statues of Gautama Buddha or religious figures is considered offensive.

3. Don’t touch religious objects casually

India has many sacred places and items connected to religions such as Hinduism and Buddhism. Always show respect around temples and idols of deities like Ganesha.

4. Don’t show public displays of affection

Holding hands is usually acceptable, but kissing or hugging in public can be seen as inappropriate.

5. Don’t refuse food too directly

If someone offers food, refusing strongly may seem rude because serving guests is a big part of Indian hospitality.
