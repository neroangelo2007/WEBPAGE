Do's in Pakistani culture

1. Dress modestly

Pakistan is generally conservative. Both men and women should wear modest clothing that covers shoulders and legs, especially when visiting mosques like Faisal Mosque in Islamabad.

2. Greet people politely

A common greeting is “Assalamualaikum” (peace be upon you). It reflects Islamic culture and is widely used in daily interactions.

3. Use the right hand

Use the right hand when eating, giving, or receiving items. The left hand is traditionally considered impolite for these actions.

4. Accept hospitality

Pakistanis are known for strong hospitality. If invited for a meal, you may be served dishes like Biryani, Nihari, or Chapli Kebab—accepting the offer is seen as respectful.

5. Remove shoes when entering homes or mosques

Always take off your shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s house.

6. Show respect during religious times

During Ramadan, avoid eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours in many areas.

Don'ts in Pakistani culture

1. Don’t shake hands with the opposite gender without permission

In conservative settings, men and women may avoid physical contact. Wait to see if the other person initiates.

2. Don’t point your feet at religious statues

Pointing feet toward statues of Gautama Buddha or religious figures is considered offensive.

3. Don’t take photos of military or government buildings

Pakistan has strict security in many areas, particularly in cities like Karachi and Lahore.

4. Don’t show public displays of affection

Holding hands is usually acceptable, but kissing or hugging in public can be seen as inappropriate.

5. Don’t refuse food repeatedly

It’s polite to decline once out of modesty, but repeated refusal may seem rude because hospitality is highly valued.

6. Don’t criticize religion or traditions openly

Religion—especially Buddhism—is deeply respected, so avoid negative remarks about religious beliefs.
